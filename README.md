# Data-analysis-project-E-commerce-Trust-Signals-vs-Price-Amazon-Marketplace-Best-Sellers

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between **trust signals** and **price positioning** among Amazon Marketplace best-selling products.

The goal is to understand:

- Do higher-priced products have stronger trust signals?
- Which trust indicators influence product positioning?
- Is price justified by perceived credibility and social proof?

This analysis focuses on Amazon Best Sellers to uncover patterns that can help e-commerce sellers optimize pricing and increase conversions.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Explore the correlation between **price** and **review metrics**
- Identify which **trust signals** differentiate premium vs. lower-priced products
- Understand how marketplace credibility affects best-seller status
- Provide data-driven insights for e-commerce strategy

---

## 🔍 Key Trust Signals Analyzed

- Average rating
- Number of reviews
- Bestseller rank

---

## 📊 Dataset Description

The dataset is scrapped on Amazon marketplace:

- Product title
- Price
- Rating
- Review count
- Category
- Ranking position
- Units sold last 30 days

## 📈 Analysis Approach

1. **Data Cleaning**
   - Handle missing values
   - Normalize price formats
   - Convert rating and review counts to numeric

2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**
   - Distribution of prices
   - Distribution of ratings
   - Correlation matrix
   - Review count vs. price scatter plots

3. **Segmentation**
   - Low vs. medium vs. high price segments
   - Trust signal comparison across segments

4. **Insights & Interpretation**
   - Identify pricing patterns
   - Detect credibility thresholds (e.g., review count benchmarks)
   - Highlight anomalies

---

## 📊 Example Research Questions

- Do higher-priced products consistently have higher ratings?
- Is there a minimum number of reviews required to compete in premium pricing?
- Are best sellers driven more by price competitiveness or social proof?
